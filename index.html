HOW TO USE
1
Enable Your Camera
When you first open CARA AR, tap "Enable Camera & Start." Your browser will ask for camera permission — tap Allow. This is required for face tracking to work. CARA AR only uses your front camera and never stores or uploads footage.
2
Import Your VRM Avatar
Tap the "Import VRM" button (bottom right) or open the Controls tray and select the VRM tab. Choose any .vrm file from your device — VRM 0.x files with ARKit morph targets work best for full expression tracking.
3
Face Your Camera
Look directly at your front camera. The green status dot at the top will turn solid when your face is detected. Your avatar will appear and mirror your head movements, blinks, mouth opens, and eyebrow raises in real time.
4
Adjust Avatar Position
Open Controls → Track tab to adjust Head Size (scale), Up/Down position, and Depth. Use these sliders to align the avatar perfectly over your face. Sensitivity and Smoothing control how responsive and fluid the tracking feels.
5
Adjust Lighting
Tap "Lighting" (bottom right) or open Controls → Light tab. Choose a preset (Studio, Warm, Cool, Night) or fine-tune individual lights with the sliders and color pickers.
6
Take a Photo
Tap the large white shutter button in the center bottom to capture a photo. It will download automatically as a PNG. The flash effect confirms your capture.
7
Top Bar Controls
The three icons at the top right let you access this guide (? icon), hide/show your avatar (eye icon), and toggle the camera background (image icon). The Neck Mask button (bottom left) fades out the avatar's body below the head for a cleaner look.
Pro Tips
• Best results in good lighting facing you
• If tracking is lost, move back to center frame
• Use Smoothing ~0.6 for cinematic feel, ~0.2 for reactive feel
• If the avatar looks off, try adjusting Head Size and Up/Down first
• Restart Camera in the Camera tab if the feed freezes
