HOW TO USE
1
Enable Your Camera
When you first open CARA AR, tap "Enable Camera & Start." Your browser will ask for camera permission — tap Allow. This is required for face tracking to work. CARA AR only uses your front camera and never stores or uploads footage.
2
Import Your VRM Avatar
Tap the "Import VRM" button (bottom right) or open the Controls tray and select the VRM tab. Choose any .vrm file from your device — VRM 0.x files with ARKit morph targets work best for full expression tracking.
3
Face Your Camera
Look directly at your front camera. The first time, it loads a high-quality AI face tracker (takes a few seconds). The green status dot turns solid when your face is detected. Your avatar then mirrors all 52 ARKit facial expressions in real time — blinks, brows, jaw, smiles, cheeks, nose, lip shapes, plus precise head pose and eye gaze, the same expression data Unity AR uses.
4
Move & Scale Your Avatar
The easiest way: drag the avatar with one finger to move it, and pinch with two fingers to make it bigger or smaller — right on the screen. For precise control, the Offset tab has Height, Depth, Side, Angle and Scale sliders, plus a Lock toggle to freeze it in place. The Track tab has Sensitivity and Smoothing to tune how the tracking feels.
5
Adjust Lighting
Tap "Lighting" (bottom right) or open Controls → Light tab. Choose a preset (Studio, Warm, Cool, Night) or fine-tune individual lights with the sliders and color pickers.
6
Take a Photo
Tap the large white shutter button in the center bottom to capture a photo. It will download automatically as a PNG. The flash effect confirms your capture.
7
Ring Light / Flash
No front light? Tap the ring icon at the top to glow a soft white border around your screen and light up your face — like a Snapchat ring light. Open Controls → Camera to adjust intensity and glow color. The glow is baked into your photos too.
8
Background Modes
Tap the image icon at the top to cycle backgrounds: Camera → Green screen → Blue screen → White → Black. Or pick directly in Controls → Camera. Use Green or Blue for chroma keying in your editor; photos capture the solid color behind your avatar.
9
Face Mesh & Flip Camera
The grid-globe icon overlays a live wireframe of your tracked face points plus a "center your head here" ring — great for lining up. The circular-arrows icon flips between front and back cameras.
10
Rotate & Offset Your Avatar
In Controls → Track you get two rotation sliders: Spin (Y) turns your avatar left/right a full 360°, and Tilt (X) rolls it forward/back. It faces you by default (180°). Tap "Face the Viewer" to reset. For finer control, the new Offset tab has Height, Depth, Side, Angle and Scale offsets, plus a "Lock Character in Place" toggle that freezes the avatar so it stops following your face — handy for setting up a fixed shot.
11
Hide UI for Streaming
Tap the small tab on the right edge of the screen to hide all buttons and controls for clean recording. The tab stays visible on the edge — tap it again to bring everything back. Your avatar keeps tracking the whole time, so it's perfect for streams and videos.
Streamer & Creator Tips
• Tap the edge tab to hide controls, tap it again to show them
• Turn on the Ring Light if your room is dark
• Use Green or Blue background to chroma-key in your editor
• Use the Rotate slider for profile or 3/4 angle shots
• Smoothing ~0.6 = cinematic, ~0.2 = snappy/reactive
• If the avatar looks off, adjust Head Size and Up/Down first
• Restart Camera in the Camera tab if the feed freezes
